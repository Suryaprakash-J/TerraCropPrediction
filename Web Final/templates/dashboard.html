Soil Type Guide

Understanding soil types and their agricultural properties

🟫

Loamy Soil

Best all-purpose agricultural soil. Balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay. Excellent water retention and drainage.

Best for: Wheat, Maize, Vegetables, Fruits
pH: 6.0 – 7.0
🔵

Clay Soil

Heavy, dense soil with excellent nutrient retention. Prone to waterlogging. Needs good drainage management.

Best for: Rice, Cotton, Sugarcane
pH: 5.5 – 7.0
🟡

Sandy Soil

Light, well-draining soil. Low nutrient retention. Requires frequent irrigation and fertilization.

Best for: Groundnut, Potato, Carrot
pH: 5.5 – 7.5
⚫

Black Soil

Rich in calcium, magnesium, and iron. Excellent moisture retention. Ideal for dry-land farming.

Best for: Cotton, Soybean, Sorghum
pH: 7.5 – 8.5
🔴

Red Soil

Rich in iron oxides. Well-drained but low in nitrogen and phosphorus. Needs fertilizer supplementation.

Best for: Coffee, Tea, Groundnut
pH: 5.5 – 7.0
🟤

Alluvial Soil

Deposited by rivers. Very fertile with good texture. Most productive agricultural soil in India.

Best for: Rice, Wheat, Sugarcane, Pulses
pH: 6.5 – 8.0
